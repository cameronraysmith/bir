\begin{block}{definition of sheaf in terms of a {\it basis}}
We can also express the sheaf condition in terms of a basis $B$ for a topology $J$ generated by $B$ on a category $\cC$ having pullbacks. If $Q = \{ f_i : c_i \rightarrow c \}_{i \in I}$ is a B-cover of $c$ a family of elements $x_i \in P(c_i)(i \in I)$ is a matching family for $Q$ if $ x_i \cdot \pi_{ij}^1 = x_j \cdot \pi_{ij}^2$ for all $i,j \in I$. $\pi^1$ and $\pi^2$ are defined as
\begin{displaymath}
\xymatrix{
c_i \times_c c_j \ar[r]^-{\pi_{ij}^2} \ar[d]_{\pi_{ij}^1} & c_j \ar[d]^{f_j} \\
c_i \ar[r]_{f_i} & c
}
\end{displaymath}
\end{block}
